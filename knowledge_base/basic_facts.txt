Velorath is a planet located approximately 1.8 astronomical units from its host star, placing it farther from its star than Earth is from the Sun. The planet has a radius roughly 0.7 times that of Earth, making it a smaller but notably dense world. Its mass is approximately 0.5 times that of Earth.

Gravity on Velorath measures 0.4g, which is about 40 percent of Earth's gravitational pull. A person weighing 70 kilograms on Earth would feel as though they weigh around 28 kilograms on Velorath. This low gravity affects how objects fall, how liquids behave, and how native life has evolved.

A single day on Velorath lasts 61 Earth hours, meaning the planet rotates much more slowly than Earth. A full year on Velorath, the time it takes to complete one orbit around its star, is equivalent to approximately 520 Earth days. The planet has a mild axial tilt of 9 degrees, resulting in weak and subtle seasonal changes compared to Earth.
