The surface of Velorath is dominated by vast flat plains composed of dark grey basaltic rock. These plains are interrupted by sharp ridge formations that rise abruptly from the ground, some reaching heights of 2 to 3 kilometers. Deep fissures cut across the terrain in several regions, some extending hundreds of meters below the surface.

There is no liquid water on the surface of Velorath. However, ice deposits have been confirmed at both polar regions and in underground caverns discovered during the 2049 mission. The underground ice exists in large cavern systems beneath the southern ridge formations and is considered one of the most significant findings for habitability research.

Near the equatorial zones, shallow pools of liquid methane have been observed. These pools are typically no more than a few meters deep and tens of meters wide. They do not support the same role water plays on Earth but are of significant scientific interest. No rivers or large liquid bodies have been identified on the surface.
