Velorath is not currently habitable for humans without significant technological support, but it holds more potential than most planets studied to date. The factors that make it a candidate for future habitation are its manageable gravity, the presence of underground water ice, existing biological life, and geothermal energy sources.

The primary barrier to habitability is the atmosphere. The high CO2 concentration and absence of breathable oxygen mean that any human presence requires fully sealed environments with independent life support. Long-term terraforming of the atmosphere is theoretically possible but would require centuries of intervention.

Temperature ranges on the surface are within survivable bounds for humans in protective gear, and the lower gravity reduces the physical demands of surface activity. Sealed habitats could realistically be built using local rock materials and powered by geothermal vents.

The existence of Duskmoss and three fauna species confirms that Velorath can sustain biological processes, which is a strong signal for habitability potential. The discovery of organic compounds in the underground ice caverns in 2049 has intensified interest in the planet's biological history. Researchers are still assessing whether these compounds are remnants of a more complex past ecosystem or indicators of ongoing subsurface biological activity.
