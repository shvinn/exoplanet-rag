Velorath hosts a category of plant-like organisms collectively referred to as Duskmoss. These organisms grow in flat, dense clusters close to the ground and rarely exceed 10 centimeters in height. They are distributed widely across the plains, particularly in areas sheltered from the strongest wind corridors.

Duskmoss has a dark blue-green pigmentation, which researchers believe is an adaptation to absorb the limited and differently-spectrally-distributed light from Velorath's host star. Unlike Earth plants, Duskmoss does not rely on photosynthesis in the traditional sense. It appears to derive energy through a combination of low-light absorption and chemosynthetic processes drawing from the mineral-rich soil.

Duskmoss produces trace amounts of oxygen as a metabolic byproduct, but the quantity is far too small to have any meaningful effect on the planet's atmospheric composition. It does not flower, produce seeds, or grow vertically. Reproduction appears to occur through lateral spreading of root-like filaments beneath the surface. It has shown resilience to temperature extremes and dust storm conditions.
