Velorath was first identified as a candidate for exploration in 2037 following long-range spectroscopic analysis that detected anomalous atmospheric composition and surface mineral signatures. The first unmanned probe, designated V-1, landed on Velorath in 2041 and transmitted surface images and basic atmospheric readings for 14 months before losing contact.

A second unmanned mission, V-2, arrived in 2044 with improved instruments. It confirmed the presence of Duskmoss and captured the first footage of Vellin creatures. V-2 also mapped the major ridge formations and identified the geothermal vent clusters near the southern fissures.

The first crewed mission arrived in 2047 with a team of six researchers. During this mission the Gralk was observed for the first time, and one encounter resulted in damage to a surface rover. The team spent 40 days on the surface before returning. Ferranite deposits were sampled and confirmed during this mission.

In 2049 a second crewed mission of eight researchers made the most significant discovery to date: a network of underground ice caverns beneath the southern ridge. Analysis of ice samples revealed trace organic compounds whose origin remains under investigation. The Shreth species was also observed for the first time near the methane pools during this mission.

A recurring low-frequency signal has been detected emanating from the southern ridge on three separate occasions across different missions. The signal does not match any known geological or atmospheric phenomenon. Its source has not been identified and remains one of the most discussed unresolved findings from Velorath exploration.
