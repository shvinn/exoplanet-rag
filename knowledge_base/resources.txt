Velorath contains several materials of significant interest to Earth exploration teams. The most notable is Ferranite, a dense magnetic mineral found in high concentrations within the ridge formations. Ferranite has an unusually strong natural magnetic field and has been flagged for potential applications in energy storage and advanced shielding technologies. Extraction would require specialized equipment due to the hardness of the surrounding rock.

The surface basaltic rock across the plains is extremely hard and dense. Early structural tests suggest it could be a viable construction material for surface habitats if processed on-site. Its thermal properties also make it suitable for insulation structures given the planet's temperature swings.

Geothermal vents located near the deep fissures release consistent heat energy from Velorath's active interior. These vents have been identified as a potential renewable energy source for any future surface installation. Several vent clusters have been mapped and their output measured as stable over the observation period.

Underground ice deposits, while primarily of scientific interest for habitability research, also represent a potential water source if extraction and purification methods can be developed. The full extent of the ice cavern systems has not yet been mapped.
