The sky on Velorath appears deep amber during daylight hours due to the scattering of light through its dense CO2-rich atmosphere. As the planet approaches dusk, the sky transitions to a dark violet before fading into a near-black night. The host star appears smaller and dimmer than Earth's Sun when viewed from the surface.

Velorath experiences frequent low-pressure storm systems that bring strong horizontal winds reaching up to 140 kilometers per hour. These storms are common across the flat plains and typically last between 6 to 10 hours. Calm periods between storms usually last 10 to 15 hours before the next system arrives.

Surface temperatures vary significantly between day and night due to the long rotation period. During peak daytime hours temperatures can reach up to 18 degrees Celsius in equatorial regions. At night temperatures drop sharply to around minus 30 degrees Celsius. Polar regions remain consistently below minus 60 degrees Celsius. There is no rainfall on Velorath in the traditional sense, but fine particulate dust storms are common during storm cycles.
